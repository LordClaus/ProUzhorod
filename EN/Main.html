About the city

Uzhhorod - a fascinating city located in the west of Ukraine, on the border with four countries: Slovakia, Poland, Hungary and Romania. Its rich historical heritage and multicultural spirit add a special flavor to this place. Uzhhorod is famous for its unique architecture, variety of cultural customs and traditions, as well as the natural beauty that surrounds it from all sides. The city offers guests and residents will have an unforgettable experience thanks to its historical and modern monuments entertainment and hospitality of his people. It is one of the main architectural monuments of the city Uzhgorodskyi the castle, which dates back to the 9th century and is one from the oldest castles on the territory of Ukraine. The castle has a unique architecture and is located on shore of the Uzh river, giving it a mesmerizing look. The city is also famous for its music and art heritage In Uzhhorod you can find numerous museums, galleries, theaters and festivals reflecting various aspects of culture and art. In general, Uzhhorod - it is a magical city that combines history, culture and nature, creating a unique atmosphere for those who visit it.

Highlights

Among the most famous places of Uzhgorod are its castle, church, synagogue and others architectural attractions. In 1646 year, under the auspices of the Vatican, the Uzhgorod Union was proclaimed and the Greek Catholic Church was formed on Transcarpathia.

History

Founded in the 9th century. The city was temporarily under the rule of Hungary, and later - Austria-Hungary. It was for a long time the fortress of the Druget family. Uzhhorod developed as an industrial and cultural center Transcarpathia. The coat of arms of Uzhgorod is officially approved by the decision of Uzhgorod city Council dated June 14, 1990. Appears itself is a German shield on a blue field, which depicts three golden vineyards growing from the ground vines, what cross each other, with three leaves and two bunches of grapes on them. Grapevine in symbol of the city has been known since 1635, when the city used a seal with a picture vine with three vines and two clusters. The coat of arms on the seal framed the inscription: "Sigillum civitatis Ungvariensis".
